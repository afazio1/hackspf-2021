FAQ

What's a Hackathon?

  • A hackathon is a multi-hour event where students with similar interests come together to work on a project. You can attend workshops, hear from people in the computer science field and network with students and reps from companies!

Will Hack SPF 2021 be virtual?

  • Due to COVID-19, the hackathon will be going completely virtual this year.

When will registration open and close?

  • Registration will open on April 1st, 2021. It will close May 30th, 2021. To be notified of these important updates, fill out the Updates Form, which is linked at the top of our website.

Do I need to know how to code to participate?

  • No! This is the perfect place for any student to create their first coding project. We'll have plenty of resources for beginners to get started! You will also have access to a support team to help with any of your concerns.

Does this cost money?

  • Nope! The event is free! Our sponsors are able to provide prizes for Hackathon winners.

Do I have to be from Scotch Plains-Fanwood to participate?

  • No! Hack SPF is open to any middle or high school student.

What materials do I need to participate?

  • Internet access is required to participate. A computer running Windows, Mac OS, or Linux is highly recommended. Chromebooks are usable, but they may not work for a few select workshops.

How do teams work?

  • At most teams can have 4 people. You can have your teammates in mind before the event, or you can find a team during our team formation activity before and after opening ceremony!

Is there a code of conduct?

  • Yes, we want to make Hack SPF enjoyable for everyone! We follow MLH's Code of Conduct: here.

Can I host a workshop?

  • Yes! We appreciate all who want to support our cause. Mentors help hackers during the event with any technical questions they might have. Applications are open now for mentors!
